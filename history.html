History of Olongapo

The earliest recorded known chronicle of Olongapo and Subic Bay was during the expeditionary and exploration feats of the Spanish Empire, the world’s colonial power during 15th to 17th century. It was called by historians as the Age of Exploration. This period was seen as the bridge between Middle Age and Modern era. Europeans, prominently the Portuguese and Spaniards pioneered long distance maritime travels In search of trading opportunities to sustain their respective monarchial societies and economies. Before the colonization of the Philippines, Olongapo was believed to be part of the vast habitat of a nomadic bands of aboriginal hunters, the Aetas.

Nodes of History
1569 - Juan de Salcedo, known as “The Last Conquistadores” and grandson of the 1st Governor and Captain General of the Philippines, Miguel Lopez de Legaspi succeeded in conquering Manila from its Muslim rulers.

1571 - The conquest continued north of Luzon Island which paved the way to the discovery of Subic Bay and later on the Ilocos Region.

1868 - 297 years later, a Spanish military expedition was dispatched to study the possibility of relocating the Cavite Naval Station in Subic Bay due to its unhealthy condition.
